The Story of Our Forests: A Call to Action

Our forests are the lungs of our planet, home to countless species, and key to our survival. But they're disappearing at an alarming rate. Let's explore the impact and how we can make a difference.

The Heated Debate: Climate Change

As our forests diminish, so does Earth's natural defense against climate change. Trees absorb CO2, but when they're cut down, that carbon is released back into the atmosphere.

  • Deforestation accounts for about 15% of all greenhouse gas emissions
  • A single tree can absorb up to 48 pounds of CO2 per year
Learn More About Forest Carbon

The Silent Extinction: Biodiversity Loss

Forests are teeming with life, hosting 80% of the world's terrestrial biodiversity. As we lose forests, we lose entire species - many before they're even discovered.

  • Up to 28,000 species may go extinct in the next quarter century due to deforestation
  • Tropical rainforests, covering just 6% of Earth's surface, are home to over half of the world's plant and animal species
